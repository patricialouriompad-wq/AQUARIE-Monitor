About AQUAIRIE

AQUAIRIE is a modular rainwater filtration prototype designed for agricultural use in farms and poultry systems. It collects and purifies rainwater through four sequential filtration layers, providing clean water for farming needs while promoting sustainability.

🏗️ The Four Filtration Layers

1

🪨 Gravel Filter — First Layer

The gravel layer is the first line of defense. It removes large debris such as leaves, twigs, insects, and coarse sediments from the collected rainwater. This prevents clogging of the finer layers below and significantly extends the lifespan of the entire filtration system.

Clean every 30 days
2

🏖️ Sand Filter — Second Layer

The sand layer filters out finer suspended particles that passed through the gravel. It improves water clarity and removes smaller sediments, dust, and microscopic particles. The sand layer plays a key role in making the water visibly cleaner before it reaches the biochar stage.

Clean every 30 days
3

🌑 Biochar Filter — Third Layer

Biochar is a highly porous, carbon-rich material produced by burning organic matter at high temperatures. In this layer, it absorbs chemical impurities, odors, and organic contaminants from the water. Biochar is particularly effective at removing pesticide residues and improving taste quality.

Replace every 60–90 days
4

🧴 Cotton Filter — Final Layer

The cotton layer is the final polishing stage. It traps very fine particles, dust, and residual sediments before the water exits the system. Cotton is the most frequently replaced filter due to its fine filtration capability and susceptibility to clogging, ensuring the cleanest possible output water.

Clean/replace every 7 days

🎯 Purpose

AQUAIRIE is designed to help small-scale farmers access clean water from rainwater harvesting. By filtering collected rainwater, it reduces dependence on commercial water sources and promotes sustainable agriculture.

📍 Target Users

Designed for rice farms, vegetable gardens, and poultry systems — especially in rural and semi-urban areas where access to clean water can be inconsistent. Ideal for small to medium agricultural operations.

🔧 Cleaning & Maintenance Guide

Follow these instructions to properly maintain each filter layer of your AQUAIRIE system.

🪨 Gravel Filter

1
Remove the gravel layer carefully from the filtration housing unit.
2
Rinse the gravel under running water to remove large debris, soil, and sediment buildup.
3
Scrub gravel gently if algae or organic growth is present.
4
Spread out in sunlight for 30 minutes to dry and disinfect naturally.
5
Return gravel to the housing, ensuring even distribution across the layer.
🗓️ Recommended: Every 30 days (1 month)

🏖️ Sand Filter

1
Remove sand carefully using a scoop; place in a clean container or bucket.
2
Rinse sand with clean water multiple times until wash water runs clear.
3
Check sand for discoloration or strong odor — replace entirely if necessary.
4
Let the sand dry completely before returning to filtration unit.
5
Repack sand evenly, ensuring no large gaps or air pockets exist in the layer.
🗓️ Recommended: Every 30 days (1 month)

🌑 Biochar Filter

1
Remove the biochar layer from the housing. Biochar cannot be washed — it must be replaced.
2
Dispose of old biochar responsibly (can be added to garden soil as a soil amendment).
3
Clean the housing section that held the biochar with water.
4
Prepare fresh biochar material — ensure it is food-grade or agricultural-grade biochar.
5
Pack the new biochar evenly into the layer compartment and seal the housing.
🗓️ Recommended: Every 60–90 days (replace, do not wash)

🧴 Cotton Filter

1
Remove the cotton filter element from its housing at the bottom of the system.
2
If lightly soiled, rinse gently with clean water and squeeze out gently (do not twist).
3
If heavily soiled, discolored, or degraded, replace with new clean cotton material.
4
Allow cotton to air dry if reusing before reinstalling into the unit.
5
Reinstall carefully, ensuring the cotton covers the full output area to prevent bypassing.
🗓️ Recommended: Every 7 days (most frequent — weekly)

💡 General Maintenance Tips

  • ✅Always use clean hands or gloves when handling filter materials.
  • ✅Log your cleaning dates in the Water Quality Log after each maintenance.
  • ✅Inspect all housing connections for cracks or leaks during each cleaning.
  • ✅If water output reduces significantly, check cotton first — it clogs fastest.
  • ✅Do not use chemical cleaners on filter materials unless specified.
